### [Aliro 1.0 Is Local-First Done Right — But Your Existing Locks Probably Won't Get It](https://smarthomedigest.com/articles/aliro-1-0-local-first-smart-lock-standard)
"Aliro is a new open standard from the Connectivity Standards Alliance (the same organization behind Matter) — but where Matter handles device control, Aliro handles credentials. It defines how a digital key stored in your phone's wallet talks to a smart lock — directly, locally, without a cloud server in the middle."

### [Local-first with Tanstack DB with Schalk Venter](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oVEczTs31yE)
"There have been various tools that help implement and manage a local-first architecture for a while now. However, with the recent beta release of TanStack DB (part of the extremely popular TanStack ecosystem), we might be on the cusp of local-first going mainstream. In this session, we will explore the principles behind local-first and how TanStack DB can be used to achieve these."

### [OpenClaw and the Architecture Nobody Noticed](https://www.distributedthoughts.org/openclaw-and-the-architecture-nobody-noticed/)
"The value in OpenClaw was in the deployment topology: local compute, local data access, familiar interfaces. That's the part that generated 209,000 stars. Not the model. Not the 'agent' capability in the abstract. The fact that it worked here, on your machine, with your stuff."

### [A Love Letter to Grist](https://alsuren.github.io/2026/03/09/a-love-letter-to-grist)
"I would not say that Grist has all of the local-first ideals nailed. I don’t think anyone does. I realised as I was using it that the data sovereignty piece is the one that I really care about though, and that is why I am all in on Grist at the moment. I would much rather have Grist with its long-now-proof sqlite storage than AFFiNE with its magical real time sync engine and no trust that I will be able to get my data out in 10 years time."
